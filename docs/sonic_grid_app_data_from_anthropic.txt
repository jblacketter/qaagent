SonicGrid Product Documentation
Application Overview
SonicGrid is a comprehensive audio content management and collaboration platform built on modern web technologies (TypeScript, Next.js, and React). The platform serves as a complete ecosystem for managing audio tracks, creating collections, facilitating artist collaborations, and delivering rich media experiences to end users. With 199 API endpoints spanning 57 feature areas, SonicGrid provides enterprise-grade capabilities for audio content creation, processing, distribution, and social interaction.

At its core, SonicGrid enables users to upload, transcode, and manage audio content while leveraging advanced AI-powered features for content generation, transcription, and enhancement. The platform integrates seamlessly with industry-standard services including Firebase for authentication and storage, AWS for media processing and delivery, and PostgreSQL for robust data persistence. Whether you're an independent artist uploading your latest track, a content creator building curated collections, or an administrator managing platform operations, SonicGrid provides the tools and workflows to support your creative and business objectives.

The platform's architecture supports multiple user roles with granular permissions, ensuring that casual listeners, content creators, and platform administrators each have appropriate access to features relevant to their needs. From simple playback sessions to complex audio remixing workflows, SonicGrid handles the technical complexity behind the scenes, allowing users to focus on creativity and content discovery. The system's extensive API surface also makes it an ideal platform for third-party integrations, enabling developers to build custom applications and automations on top of SonicGrid's robust infrastructure.

Feature Descriptions
Core Content Management
Tracks Management Upload, organize, and manage your audio content with comprehensive track management tools. Create new tracks by uploading audio files or providing URLs, edit metadata including titles, descriptions, and artwork, and organize your catalog with tags and categories. The system automatically handles audio transcoding to ensure optimal playback across all devices and connection speeds. Track 25 different operations including bulk uploads, metadata updates, and deletion workflows.

Collections Organize tracks into curated collections such as albums, playlists, or thematic groupings. Create public collections to share with your audience or private collections for personal organization. Collections support full CRUD operations, allowing you to add or remove tracks, reorder content, update collection artwork and descriptions, and manage visibility settings. Perfect for artists releasing albums or curators building themed playlists.

Meta Content Enhance your audio content with rich metadata including lyrics, liner notes, credits, and supplementary information. The Meta Content feature supports creating, reading, updating, and deleting associated content that provides context and depth to your tracks. Link multiple meta content items to individual tracks or entire collections to create immersive listening experiences.

Profiles & Artists Every user maintains a comprehensive profile showcasing their activity, uploaded content, and collections. Artist profiles provide dedicated spaces for musicians and creators to present their work, biography, and discography. Browse artist catalogs, discover new creators, and follow your favorites to stay updated on new releases.

Connections Build your network by creating connections with other users, artists, and collaborators. Manage your connections through a full suite of operations including sending connection requests, accepting or declining invitations, viewing your network, and removing connections. Connections enable collaboration features and personalized content recommendations.

Messages Communicate directly with other platform users through the integrated messaging system. Send private messages, manage conversations, mark messages as read, and maintain your inbox. The messaging feature supports real-time communication for collaboration, feedback, and community building.

Activity Feed Stay informed about platform activity through personalized activity feeds. View updates from your connections, track engagement on your content, and discover trending tracks and collections. The activity system tracks listens, shares, and interactions to keep you connected with your community.

Content Discovery & Playback

Text Refinement & Summarization Enhance written content with AI-powered text refinement that improves clarity, grammar, and style. Generate concise summaries of longer text content for descriptions, social media posts, and promotional materials.

Upload audio files directly from your device, provide URLs for remote content, or use specialized upload endpoints for images and videos. The system handles file validation, virus scanning, and format conversion automatically.

Remix & Remixing Workflows Upload audio content specifically for remixing purposes, enabling collaborative creation workflows. The platform supports streaming remixes and provides tools for combining multiple audio sources into new creative works.

Proxy Services Access images and videos through proxy endpoints that handle caching, resizing, and format conversion on-the-fly. Proxy services ensure fast content delivery while protecting original asset URLs and enabling dynamic transformations.

Download Management Provide authorized users with download access to high-quality audio files. The download system tracks usage, enforces permissions, and supports resumable downloads for large files.

User Journeys
Journey 1: Artist Onboarding & First Track Upload
Persona: Independent musician releasing their first single on SonicGrid

Sign Up for Account: Navigate to the registration page and create a new account by providing email, password, and basic profile information. The system sends a verification email to confirm account ownership.

Complete Artist Profile: After email verification, log in and access the profile settings. Upload a profile photo, write an artist biography, add social media links, and specify musical genres. This information helps listeners discover and connect with your work.

Upload First Track: Navigate to the track upload interface. Select an audio file from your device (or provide a URL to hosted audio). The system validates the file format and begins uploading. While uploading, fill in track metadata including title, description, genre tags, and release date.

Add Track Artwork: Upload custom artwork for the track or use the AI-powered image generation tool to create artwork based on the track's audio characteristics and your artistic preferences.

Configure Track Settings: Set the track's visibility (public, private, or unlisted), enable or disable downloads, and configure licensing information. Choose whether to allow remixing by other platform users.

Wait for Processing: The platform automatically transcodes the audio into multiple formats and bitrates. Monitor the processing status through the dashboard. Processing typically completes within minutes depending on track length.

Preview & Publish: Once processing completes, preview the track using the integrated player. Verify that metadata, artwork, and audio quality meet your standards. When satisfied, publish the track to make it discoverable on the platform.

Share & Promote: Copy the track's shareable link and share it on social media. The track now appears in your artist profile and becomes discoverable through search and recommendation algorithms.

Journey 2: Curator Creating a Themed Collection
Persona: Music enthusiast building a curated playlist for a specific mood or occasion

Browse & Discover: Use the search and feed features to discover tracks matching your collection theme. Search by genre, mood tags, or artist names. Listen to previews and add promising tracks to a temporary list.

Create New Collection: Navigate to the Collections area and click "Create New Collection." Provide a collection name, description, and theme tags. Upload collection artwork or generate it using AI tools.

Add Tracks to Collection: Search for specific tracks or browse your temporary list. Add tracks to the collection one by one or in bulk. The system allows adding tracks you've uploaded as well as tracks from other artists (respecting their sharing settings).

Organize Track Order: Drag and drop tracks to arrange them in your preferred sequence. Consider flow, energy levels, and thematic progression when ordering tracks. Preview the collection to ensure smooth transitions.

Enhance with Meta Content: Add liner notes explaining the collection's theme, track selection rationale, or background stories. Create a rich listening experience by providing context for your curation choices.

Configure Sharing Settings: Choose whether the collection is public (discoverable by all users), unlisted (accessible only via direct link), or private (visible only to you). Enable collaborative features to allow other users to suggest tracks.

Publish & Share: Publish the collection to make it available according to your sharing settings. Share the collection link on social media, in messages to connections, or through the platform's activity feed.

Maintain & Update: Return to the collection periodically to add new tracks, remove outdated content, or refresh the description. Monitor engagement metrics to see how listeners interact with your curation.

Journey 3: Listener Discovering & Engaging with Content
Persona: Music fan exploring new artists and building their library

Browse Home Feed: After logging in, view the personalized home feed showcasing new releases from followed artists, trending tracks, and algorithmic recommendations based on listening history.

Search for Specific Content: Use the search feature to find a specific artist, track, or genre. Enter search terms and filter results by content type (tracks, collections, artists, users).

Explore Artist Profile: Click on an artist's name to view their complete profile. Browse their discography, read their biography, view their connections, and see their activity on the platform.

Start Playback Session: Click play on a track to begin listening. The playback session initializes, tracking your listening for analytics while providing seamless audio streaming. Use playback controls to pause, skip, or adjust volume.

Engage with Content: While listening, like the track to add it to your favorites, share it with connections via messages, or post it to your activity feed. Leave comments or feedback for the artist.

Create Connection: If you enjoy an artist's work, send them a connection request. Once accepted, you'll receive updates about their new releases and activity in your feed.

Build Personal Collection: Create your own collection of favorite tracks. Add tracks from various artists to build a personalized playlist for different moods, activities, or occasions.

Explore Recommendations: Based on your listening history and engagement patterns, the platform suggests similar artists, tracks, and collections. Explore these recommendations to discover new music aligned with your tastes.

Review Listening History: Access your listening history to rediscover tracks you've enjoyed. View statistics about your most-played artists, genres, and tracks.

Journey 4: Collaborative Interview Production
Persona: Podcast producer creating an interview episode with transcription and metadata

Upload Interview Audio: Navigate to the upload interface and select the recorded interview audio file. Provide basic metadata including episode title, guest name, and recording date.

Initiate Transcription: Once the audio upload completes, trigger the audio transcription service. Choose between standard transcription (higher accuracy, longer processing) or simple transcription (faster, suitable for clear audio).

Review Transcript: When transcription completes, review the generated text for accuracy. Edit any misrecognized words or phrases using the meta content editing interface.

Add Show Notes: Create meta content entries for show notes, including timestamps for key discussion points, links to resources mentioned, and guest biography. Link these meta content items to the interview track.

Generate Promotional Assets: Use the image generation feature to create episode artwork based on the interview content and guest information. Generate waveform visualizations for social media promotion.

Create Video Visualizer: Use the video generation feature to create a visualizer video combining the audio with the generated artwork and waveform. This video can be shared on video platforms.

Summarize Content: Use the text summarization tool to generate a concise episode description from the full transcript. Refine the summary using the text refinement feature to ensure clarity and engagement.

Publish Episode: Configure the interview track's visibility settings, add it to your podcast collection, and publish. The track now includes audio, transcript, show notes, artwork, and video assets.

Share Across Channels: Download the video visualizer for YouTube, copy the shareable link for social media, and send direct messages to connections who might be interested in the episode.

Journey 5: Administrator Managing Platform Operations
Persona: Platform administrator monitoring system health and moderating content

Access Admin Dashboard: Log in with administrator credentials and navigate to the admin dashboard. View high-level platform metrics including active users, recent uploads, and system health indicators.

Monitor System Health: Check the health endpoint to verify all platform services are operational. Review debug information if any issues are detected. Monitor integration status for Firebase, AWS, and database connections.

Review Recent Content: Browse recently uploaded tracks and collections to ensure compliance with platform guidelines. Use admin read operations to view content details, metadata, and user information.

Moderate Flagged Content: Review content that has been flagged by users or automated systems. Listen to tracks, read descriptions, and assess whether content violates platform policies.

Take Moderation Action: For policy violations, use admin update operations to mark content as private, add warning labels, or use admin delete operations to remove content entirely. Document moderation decisions for audit trails.

Manage User Accounts: Review user accounts that have received complaints or exhibit suspicious behavior. Use user management operations to view account details, update permissions, or suspend accounts if necessary.

Configure Feature Flags: Access the feature flags interface to enable or disable platform features. Toggle flags to roll out new features gradually, disable problematic features, or run A/B tests.

Update Site Settings: Modify platform-wide settings including navigation structure, branding elements, and integration configurations. Changes to site settings affect all users immediately.

Manage Email Communications: Configure email templates for transactional messages, send platform announcements, and review email delivery metrics. Ensure users receive important notifications about their accounts and content.

Review Analytics: Access comprehensive analytics about platform usage, content engagement, and user behavior. Use insights to inform product decisions and identify areas for improvement.

Perform Maintenance Tasks: Execute specialized admin operations like fixing content view metadata, triggering batch transcoding jobs, or managing S3 storage objects. These operations maintain platform health and data integrity.

Create and manage their own profile
Upload tracks and create collections
Search and discover content across the platform
Play audio content and manage playback sessions
Send and receive messages with other users
Create and manage connections with other users
View their own activity and listening history
Like, share, and comment on content
Create meta content for their own tracks
Generate AI-powered content (images, transcriptions, summaries)
Download content where permitted by creators
Update their own content and metadata
What They Cannot Do:

Access other users' private content without permission
Delete other users' content or accounts
Modify platform-wide settings
Access administrative dashboards or analytics
Manage feature flags or system configurations
Perform bulk operations on content they don't own
Access debug or health monitoring endpoints

What They Can Do:

All capabilities available to regular users
Access comprehensive admin dashboard with platform-wide analytics
Create, read, update, and delete any content across the platform
Manage user accounts including creation, modification, and deletion
Configure site settings and platform-wide parameters
Manage feature flags to enable/disable features dynamically
Access system health and debug information
Moderate content and enforce community guidelines
Configure and send platform email communications
Manage integrations with external services (Firebase, AWS)

View detailed analytics and usage metrics
Manage S3 storage and media assets
Configure navigation and UI elements
Access all 37 admin-specific operations

Access the platform without proper authentication
Bypass audit logging for sensitive operations
Modify system code or infrastructure directly (requires developer access)
API Consumer Role
Who: External systems, third-party applications, and developers integrating with SonicGrid programmatically

What They Can Do:

Authenticate using API tokens or keys
Read public content including tracks, collections, and artist profiles
Search and discover content programmatically
Create content on behalf of authenticated users (with proper authorization)
Update content owned by the associated user account
Access health check endpoints for monitoring
Retrieve metadata and content information
Initiate playback sessions and track listens
Upload content via API endpoints
Trigger content processing operations (transcoding, transcription)
What They Cannot Do:

Access private user data without explicit authorization
Perform administrative operations without admin-level API credentials
Bypass rate limiting or usage quotas
Access endpoints requiring interactive authentication (OAuth flows)
Delete content without proper ownership verification
Modify platform settings or configurations
Access debug endpoints (reserved for internal use)
